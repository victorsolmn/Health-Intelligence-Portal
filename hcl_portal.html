Silent Disease Discovery

Undiagnosed Chronic Disease Surfacing

Identifies the "hidden sick" — employees with abnormal AHC lab results but zero corresponding ICD-10 diagnosis in claims. 1 in 4 employees under 35 are affected.

Key Value

Early intervention ROI by catching pre-diabetic and hypertensive markers before claims occur. 412 employees at TechCorp = ₹38.4L projected exposure.

Features

  • Two-stage PU Learning — not standard supervised
  • South Asian-specific clinical thresholds
  • Automated wellness nudges · Aggregate output only
Why PU Learning: "No ICD-10 claim" ≠ healthy — it's a missing label, not a negative. Standard supervised learning treats undiagnosed as healthy (wrong). PU Learning trains on confirmed positives vs unlabelled.
Stack: Python · XGBoost · PU-learn · MLflow · Annual AHC cycle trigger
Privacy: Individual scores go to employee wellness app only — never HR. Employer sees aggregate counts per condition category. DPDP compliant.
Validation: Precision@100 target ≥40% vs baseline 8% (random).

Causal ROI Engine

Statistical Attribution

Causal inference to prove actual claims savings from wellness programmes — not naïve before/after, which inflates ROI by 2.5×. Identifies which sub-groups truly benefit.

Key Value

Moves beyond correlation to show CFOs the actual ₹ saved per participant with 95% CI. ₹1.2Cr validated vs ₹3.1Cr naïve estimate. Validation Institute ready.

Features

  • Difference-in-Differences (DiD)
  • Causal Forests / EconML for sub-group CATE
  • DoWhy + CausalML frameworks
Three methods: DiD (ongoing programmes), Synthetic Control (new rollouts), Causal Forests/CATE (which cohorts benefit most).
Stack: Python · EconML · DoWhy · CausalML · MLflow · Semi-annual refresh
Privacy: DiD operates on cohort-level aggregates. Minimum 30 employees per CATE cell (k-anonymity). Tripartite DPA for claims outcome data.
Citation: Jones, Molitor & Reif (2019) Illinois Workplace Wellness Study.
